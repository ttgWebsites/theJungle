Book Summary

  • So basically...
  • Upton Sinclair went undercover in 1904 working in a Chicago meatpacking plant to write The Jungle. His intentions were to portray an immigrant's life in the US, however many saw the focus of this novel as the corruption and terrible conditions of the American meatpacking industry. The novel uses harsh tones to describe the poverty and unpleasant living and working conditions of the American working class. The novel helped make people aware of the issues of the poverty-stricken working class known, as well as the corruption in the meat industry.
