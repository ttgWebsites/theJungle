Basic Info

  • Title
  • The Jungle
  • Author
  • Upton Sinclair
  • Published
  • The novel was first published in 1906 by Sinclair himself as no one would pick it up at first.
  • Genre
  • Social criticism or Political fiction.
  • Setting
  • The novel is set in the early 1900's in a place known as Packingtown, a district in Chicago where the meat packing industries were located.
  • Point Of View
  • The novel is told in the third person from an unspecified narrator. The narrator mainly narrates on Jurgis' perspective as he struggles in his new life in America.
  • Conflict
  • Jurgis brings his family to America because of the American dream, however from early on he as well as his family struggle to get by due to the meat packing industries horrible treatment of its employees. They make barely any in wages, are subject to terrible unsanitary conditions, and have no support. The family is often dealing with hunger, at some points homelessness, and even death.
